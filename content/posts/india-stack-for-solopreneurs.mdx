every week some founder posts on linkedin "building on india stack" and it just means they added a razorpay button.

i did the exact same thing. so i am not judging. just observing.

upi is everywhere now. 15 billion transactions last month. i read that number and my brain stops. like what does 15 billion even look like. but the point is if your product does not accept upi in 2026 you are not a business. you are a hobby.

i used razorpay. took one afternoon. the documentation is fine. not great. fine.

but here is the thing.

## everyone is building the same stuff

go to product hunt. every week there is a new indian fintech startup. "upi for x" "upi for y". upi for dogs. upi for plants.

stop.

upi is solved. move on.

the interesting stuff is in the layers nobody is building for.

## account aggregators

nobody talks about this. at meetups. on twitter. nowhere.

but this is the one that actually changes things for a small seller in india.

right now if a bakery owner in kochi wants a 50k loan she has to go to the bank. print 6 months of statements. wait 3 days. maybe get rejected.

with aa framework she can share 24 months of transaction history from her phone. one click. lenders can see real cash flow not self declared income.

we are looking at this for makeforme. imagine a dashboard that says "you qualify for 75k based on your orders. want it?" that is not possible in the us. here it is.

why is nobody building this.

## ondc

great idea. messy execution.

if you are building for indian sellers watch it. play with it. but do not bet your company on it yet.

when upi launched everyone said it would fail. look now. ondc might take longer. the direction is clear. the timeline is not.

## what i actually think

indian founders are obsessed with copying the us. "stripe for india" "plaid for india". stop.

the us has stripe and plaid. india has something better. a government that actually built infrastructure that works. aadhaar. upi. aa. gstn.

the winners will build on top of these rails for indian users. not ports of us products with an indian payment button.

at makeforme every decision starts with "will this work for a seller in tier 2 who runs her business from a phone"

most founders ask the wrong question.

i am trying to ask the right one.
