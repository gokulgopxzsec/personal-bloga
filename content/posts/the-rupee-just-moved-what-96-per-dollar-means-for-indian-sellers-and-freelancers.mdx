96.27.

live number right now. moneycontrol closed yesterday at 96.28, up 7 paise after a four-day slide. sensex rallied 965 points same session. surface-level, things look fine.

surface-level means nothing when you're the one paying the bills.

## every dollar-denominated tool just got pricier

shopify. facebook ads. adobe creative cloud. any saas subscription, really. all more expensive today than last month.

at 80 rupees per dollar, a $29 tool cost ₹2,320. at 96, ₹2,784. a rounding error? absolutely not. that's a new raw material, an hour of work you now need to account for.

makeforme charges ₹99 a month. always has, deliberately. we eat the forex risk, not the seller. built it that way after watching a friend lose handmade jewellery orders in instagram DMs and realizing she needed something dead simple.

most tools you use daily aren't built like that.

## the part everyone forgets

a weaker rupee makes your product cheaper for someone holding dollars.

sell a ₹500 kurta to a buyer in texas? costs them about $5.20 instead of $6.25 now. real competitive advantage, real volume potential.

here's the catch though: actually selling internationally means handling cross-border payments, conversion fees, and shipping that doesn't eat your margin alive. most small sellers i know, 90% of customers are indian. so that export upside stays theoretical while tool cost increases land in your inbox tomorrow morning.

## what the market data actually tells us

moneycontrol's been tracking the nifty between 23,800 and 24,300 all week. indecision, not strength. rupee snapping a losing streak today feels like a pause, not a reversal.

stock-level, ECLERX up 12.5%, FEDERALBNK up 6.8%, AIIL nearly 10%. IT companies and banks rallying. investors betting on global demand for indian services paid in dollars. broad-based confidence? nah, just smart money flowing to the obvious plays.

real data for a seller isn't the sensex. it's the margin on your last hundred orders. did it shrink? did you raise prices? those numbers matter and the rest is noise.

## so what do you actually do

stop checking the forex rate daily. meaningless at that frequency. monthly is enough.

audit your tools. every subscription you pay for in dollars. calculate total monthly exposure. if it's significant, find a rupee-priced alternative. for predictability, not just savings. can't plan a business when costs jump 5% every quarter.

honestly, this is also the moment to think about your customer base. nobody's saying pivot to exports overnight. but adding a paypal button to your site, listing one product on an international marketplace, even a small dollar income stream starts offsetting dollar-denominated costs.

pricing with confidence matters too. costs went up, your prices need to follow. don't be shy about it. "due to recent increases in operational costs" is a perfectly valid thing to put in a story or post. your real customers understand. anyone who only buys because you're cheapest was never loyal anyway.

## why local-first actually matters

96 per dollar is a headline number. for most of us it's slow, persistent pressure. the kind that erodes margins over months, not days.

exactly why building products priced in indian rupees for the indian market isn't just nice philosophy. it's survival logic. why i'll keep fighting to hold makeforme at ₹99. stability matters more than the number. nobody should have to become a forex analyst to run a small business.

dollar goes up, dollar goes down. your focus stays on product, customer, craft. let the banks handle the rest.
