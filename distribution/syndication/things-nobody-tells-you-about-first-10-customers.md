so i thought getting the first 10 customers would be the hard part.

turns out keeping them is harder. in a way i did not expect.

## they will find bugs

not like "oh this button is broken" bugs.

like "i entered my phone number and the entire page turned blue" bugs.

one seller sent me a screenshot and said "what is this". i looked at it for 20 minutes before realizing it was a css bug that only happened on her specific phone model with a specific screen size at a specific zoom level.

i fixed it but i still dont know how she found it.

## they will ask for things

"can you add COD"

"can you add a way to schedule orders for delivery on tuesday because i only go to the post office on tuesdays"

"can you make the font bigger my mother uses this too"

"can you add marathi"

i built zero of these things. not because i dont want to. because i am one person and i have to choose.

i told the marathi lady "not yet" and she said "okay". and she still uses the product. turns out people are okay with "not yet" as long as you are honest about it.

## they will pay late

not intentionally. they just forget.

one seller paid me after 3 weeks and said "sorry i forgot lol".

i said "no problem" and meant it.

because the alternative is chasing them and being annoying and ruining the relationship. and 99 rupees a month is not worth ruining a relationship over.

## the weirdest thing

they care about things i did not care about.

i spent weeks perfecting the checkout flow. making it smooth. reducing clicks.

then one seller told me she loves the store because "the color is nice".

the color. i picked it randomly. it took 2 seconds.

she did not notice the checkout flow. she noticed the color.

so now i spend less time on the things i think matter and more time asking them what they see.

because i am building this for them. not for me.

## the first 10

they are not customers. they are collaborators.

they tell you when something is broken. they tell you what they need. they forgive you when things go wrong.

if you treat them like users you lose them.

if you treat them like people they stay.

sounds obvious. took me 2 months to learn.
