*Originally published on [my blog](https://blog.gokul.me/blog/building-makeforme-in-public).*

shruthi called me at 11pm again. she always calls late. i think that's when she finally sits down after packing orders and feeding her cat and all the things.

she said "i lost three orders this week i swear i wrote them down but i cant find the notebook"

okay let me build something. a page. one link. just put your earrings there and people can click and pay. no more notebook.

that was four months ago.

13 sellers now. two months since the first one went live. not 13 in four months. 13 in two months. the first two months was just me building stuff and showing it to shruthi and her saying "no this is confusing" and me going back and fixing it.

two of the 13 sellers i have never met. they just found it. someone sent them a link and they clicked and signed up.

that specific thing. someone sending a link. that is the only thing that works.

## i spent money on ads

₹1,978.

this is embarrassing to type but here goes.

789 clicks.

₹0 in orders.

not a single signup. not a single "what is this" message. nothing.

shruthi saw the ad on her feed and messaged me "lol i saw your ad". that was the entire result of my paid marketing strategy. one lol from a friend.

i dont even know what i was thinking. "small business owners in india" is not a target audience. it is 50 million people. what was i expecting.

shyam runs a tiffin service in gachibowli. he told me later "i saw your ad. thought it was a scam. then my friend sent me your link and i tried it."

so the ad actually worked. just not the way i wanted. it planted a seed. the harvest happened months later through word of mouth.

maybe ads are not useless. maybe i am just impatient.

## what i notice about the sellers

once they put their link on their packaging they are not leaving. not because the product is amazing. because that link is printed on boxes sitting in customers homes. changing it means throwing away boxes.

the switching cost is physical. i did not plan this. it just happened.

one seller in kochi. brownies. three types. that's her entire catalogue. classic. walnut. dark chocolate. 15-20 orders a month through the store and she makes more selling brownies than i make from the platform.

i am not even mad. i am studying her.

she posts every day at 6pm. her photos are just phone pictures on a kitchen counter. no fancy lighting. no flat lays. just brownies on a plate.

and people buy them.

maybe that is the lesson. not every seller needs a perfect store. they just need a store that works. on a phone. at 6pm when they are tired and hungry and scrolling.

## what my day looks like

5am wake up. check if any stores broke overnight. they usually did not. fix small things. message sellers. build features they ask for.

no vc money. no co-founder. no office.

just me and a macbook air with a cracked screen corner that i keep meaning to fix but never do.

one time i was on a call with a seller and my battery died and she called me back and said "you should charge your phone" and i felt like a child.

## why write this

every indian founder i see posts fake numbers. "crossed 1cr arr in month one!" no you did not. your dad invested and you called it revenue.

i hate that.

so here is the truth. 13 sellers. 2 months. zero from ads.

if this fails you will see it here. if it works you will see that too.

see you whenever.
